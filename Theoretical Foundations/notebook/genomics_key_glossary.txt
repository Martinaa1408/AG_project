----Core Genetics & DNA Basics----

Gene → DNA segment coding for a functional RNA or protein.

Allele → Variant form of a gene.

Locus → Chromosomal position of a gene/marker.

Genotype → Genetic makeup (allele combination).

Phenotype → Observable trait; product of genotype × environment.

Homozygous/Heterozygous → Same vs different alleles at a locus.

Dominant/Recessive → Dominant masks recessive in heterozygotes.

Haploid/Diploid/Polyploid → Sets of chromosomes (n, 2n, >2n).

Chromosome → DNA + protein structure carrying genetic info.

Karyotype → Ordered representation of all chromosomes.

Genome → Full genetic content (nuclear + organellar).

Epigenome → Chemical modifications regulating gene expression.

Cytogenetics → Microscopy-based study of chromosomes (banding, FISH).


----Classical & Transmission Genetics----

Mendel’s Laws:

Segregation → alleles separate equally into gametes.

Independent assortment → alleles of different genes segregate independently (if not linked).

Genetic cross → Controlled mating to study inheritance.

Test cross → Cross with homozygous recessive to determine genotype.

Monohybrid/Dihybrid/Trihybrid ratios → 3:1, 9:3:3:1, etc.

Linkage → Genes close together inherited together.

Crossing-over → Homologous chromosome exchange (meiosis I).

Centimorgan (cM) → Recombination frequency unit (1% = 1 cM).

Sex linkage → Gene on X/Y; different inheritance patterns.

Pseudoautosomal regions (PAR) → X–Y recombining regions.

Pedigree → Diagram of inheritance across generations.

Consanguinity → Related mating; increases homozygosity.

----Molecular Biology & Techniques----

DNA replication → Semi-conservative copying of DNA.

PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) → Amplification of specific DNA sequences.

Sanger sequencing → Chain-termination sequencing method.

Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) → High-throughput parallel sequencing.

Pyrosequencing → Sequencing by detecting pyrophosphate light signal.

Illumina sequencing → Sequencing-by-synthesis + bridge amplification.

Emulsion PCR → Bead-based PCR (454, SOLiD).

Library preparation → DNA fragmentation + adapter ligation.

Electropherogram/Pyrogram → Visualization of Sanger/pyrosequencing data.

FASTQ → Reads + Q-scores; FASTA → sequence only.

BAM/VCF/BED → Alignment, variant, and feature formats.


----Sequencing & Assembly Metrics----

Read → Single raw sequence from sequencer.

Coverage (Depth) → Avg. times each base is sequenced.

Breadth of coverage → Fraction of target genome covered.

GC content → % of G/C bases, affects sequencing bias.

N50 → Contig length where 50% of assembly is in equal/longer contigs.

Phred Q-score → Error probability metric (Q20=1%, Q30=0.1%).

Scaffold → Ordered contigs linked by paired reads.

Reference genome → Standard sequence assembly used for alignment.


----Population Genetics----

Population → Interbreeding group of individuals.

Gene pool → All alleles present in population.

Allele frequency (p, q) → Frequency of a given allele.

Genotype frequency → Frequency of a specific genotype.

Hardy–Weinberg Equilibrium (HWE) → Null model for allele/genotype frequencies (no evolution).

Evolutionary forces → Mutation, migration (gene flow), drift, selection.

Effective population size (Ne) → Size of idealized population with same genetic drift as real population.

Heterozygosity (HO, HE) → Observed vs expected heterozygosity.

Inbreeding coefficient (F) → Probability alleles are identical by descent.

Pedigree inbreeding coefficient (Fped) → F from known family structure.

Runs of Homozygosity (ROH) → Continuous homozygous tracts.

Fixation index (FST) → Differentiation between populations (0–1).

Linkage disequilibrium (LD) → Non-random association of alleles; basis of haplotypes.

Haplotype → Alleles at multiple loci inherited together.

Wahlund effect → Substructure reduces heterozygosity.

Genetic drift → Random allele frequency changes in finite populations.

Founder effect/Bottleneck → Drift caused by small population size.


----Quantitative Genetics----

Quantitative trait → Polygenic + environmental influence; continuous distribution.

Phenotypic variance (VP) → Total observed variation.

Genetic variance (VG) → Variation due to genetics.

Additive variance (VA) → Transmissible genetic variance.

Dominance variance (VD) → Interaction between alleles at a locus.

Environmental variance (VE) → Non-genetic contribution to variation.

Heritability (H², h²) → Proportion of VP explained by genetics (broad vs narrow sense).

Selection differential (S) → Difference between selected mean and population mean.

Response to selection (R) → Expected change in mean next generation.

GxE interaction → Phenotype influenced by genotype × environment.

Breeding value → Genetic contribution to offspring phenotype.


----Statistics & Bioinformatics----

p-value → Probability of observed data under null.

Multiple testing → Many tests increase false positives.

Bonferroni correction → α / number of tests (strict).

FDR (False Discovery Rate) → Proportion of false positives among significant results.

q-value → Adjusted significance level controlling FDR.

GWAS (Genome-Wide Association Study) → Test SNP-trait associations genome-wide.

Manhattan plot → –log10(p) vs chromosomal position (GWAS visualization).

CNV (Copy Number Variation) → DNA gain/loss events.

Structural variation → Inversions, translocations, duplications, deletions.

Annotation → Linking genomic regions to biological meaning (genes, features).

Variant calling → Identifying SNPs/indels from aligned reads.


----Omics & Applied Genomics----

Genomics → Study of entire genome sequences.

Transcriptomics → Study of RNA transcripts.

Proteomics → Study of expressed proteins.

Metabolomics → Study of small metabolites.

Epigenomics → Genome-wide epigenetic modification profiling.

Phenomics → High-throughput phenotyping.

Functional genomics → Connecting sequence to biological function (gene knockout, RNA-seq, etc.).

Comparative genomics → Comparing species’ genomes to study evolution/function.

Hybrid sequencing → Combining technologies (short + long reads).

Pangenome → Core + accessory genes across populations.

Metagenomics → Sequencing environmental DNA to study communities.

Single-cell genomics → Sequencing at single-cell resolution.


----Project-Linked Concepts (Applied Examples)----

Assembly → Building a genome from reads (de novo or reference-guided).

Annotation pipeline → Structural + functional gene annotation.

BUSCO score → Benchmarking assembly completeness (single-copy orthologs).

Synteny → Conserved gene order between species.

CAZy classification → Carbohydrate-active enzymes (e.g., PLA degradation genes).

Hybrid approach → PacBio/ONT (long reads) + Illumina (short reads).

DEG (Differentially Expressed Genes) → Genes with significant expression change.

Phylogenetics → Evolutionary tree reconstruction from sequence data.

Comparative genomics in biotechnology → Identify pathways for biodegradation, metabolism, etc.

Sample Collection (cells, tissue, environment)
    ↓
DNA or RNA Extraction
    ↓
Quality Control (QC: quantity, purity, integrity)
    ↓
Choice of Sequencing Strategy
    ↓
┌───────────────────────── Sequencing Branches ─────────────────────────┐

[1] Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS)
    → DNA fragmentation → Adapter ligation
    → PCR amplification (optional)
    → Platform:
         - Illumina (short reads, high accuracy)
         - PacBio SMRT (long reads)
         - Oxford Nanopore (long reads, portable)
    → Alignment or de novo assembly → Annotation

[2] Whole Exome Sequencing (WES)
    → DNA fragmentation → Adapter ligation
    → Exome capture probes (hybridization-based enrichment)
    → PCR → Illumina sequencing → Variant calling

[3] Targeted Sequencing
    → PCR panels or hybrid-capture panels
    → Illumina sequencing → Focused variant detection

[4] RNA-seq (Transcriptome)
    → RNA extraction → rRNA depletion / polyA selection
    → Reverse transcription (RNA → cDNA)
    → Adapter ligation → Sequencing (Illumina or ONT)
    → Transcript quantification, DEG analysis

[5] ChIP-seq (Chromatin Immunoprecipitation)
    → Crosslink protein-DNA → Immunoprecipitate with antibody
    → Reverse crosslink → DNA purification
    → Library prep → Sequencing
    → Peak calling → TF binding sites & epigenetics

[6] Methyl-seq (Bisulfite Sequencing)
    → Bisulfite treatment (C → U unless methylated)
    → Library prep → Sequencing
    → Basecalling → Methylation maps

[7] aCGH (Array Comparative Genomic Hybridization)
    → Label sample & reference DNA with different dyes
    → Co-hybridize to array → Measure fluorescence ratio
    → Copy number variation (CNV) detection (no NGS)

    → Variant Calling, Expression profiling, CNV/SV analysis

ILLUMINA (SBS):
DNA → Fragmentation → End repair + A-tailing → Adapter ligation
    → Denature & Bind to Flow Cell
    → Bridge Amplification (cluster generation)
    → Sequencing-by-Synthesis (4-color, reversible terminators)
    → Imaging cycles → Basecalling → FASTQ

OXFORD NANOPORE (ONT):
DNA/RNA → Minimal fragmentation (optional) → Adapter ligation (motor protein)
    → Load onto Nanopore Flow Cell
    → Strand passes through nanopore → Current signal
    → Real-time basecalling → FASTQ

ION TORRENT:
DNA → Fragmentation → Adapter ligation → Emulsion PCR (beads)
    → Beads in microwells
    → Nucleotide incorporation → pH change detection
    → Signal processed → FASTQ

ROCHE 454 (Pyrosequencing, DISCONTINUED):
DNA → Fragmentation → Adapter ligation → Emulsion PCR (beads)
    → Beads in picotiter plate wells
    → Nucleotide incorporation → Light emission
    → Imaging → Basecalling

PACBIO SMRT:
DNA → SMRTbell library (circular DNA)
    → Load into Zero-Mode Waveguides (ZMWs)
    → Real-time polymerase synthesis → Fluorescent pulses detected
    → Long reads (HiFi after CCS) → FASTQ

SOLiD (DISCONTINUED):
DNA → Fragmentation → Adapter ligation → Emulsion PCR
    → Beads fixed on slide
    → Sequencing-by-ligation with fluorescent probes
    → Color-space reads → Conversion to FASTQ

GWAS:
1. Sample Collection
   ↓
2. DNA Extraction & QC
   ↓
3. Genotyping
   - SNP arrays OR sequencing
   ↓
4. Data Preprocessing
   - Format conversion (VCF, PLINK files)
   - Sample/marker filtering
   ↓
5. Quality Control (QC)
   - Call rate filtering
   - Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium check
   - Minor allele frequency (MAF) filtering
   - Relatedness/sex checks
   ↓
6. Imputation
   - Infer missing genotypes using reference panels
   ↓
7. Population Structure Analysis
   - PCA, kinship matrix, genomic inflation check
   ↓
8. Association Testing
   - Logistic or linear regression per SNP
   - Mixed models for population structure
   ↓
9. Multiple Testing Correction
   - Bonferroni or FDR (p < 5×10⁻⁸ typical threshold)
   ↓
10. Visualization
    - Manhattan & QQ plots
   ↓
11. Biological Interpretation
    - Identify candidate genes, pathways
